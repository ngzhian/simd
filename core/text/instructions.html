Instructions¶

Instructions are syntactically distinguished into plain and structured instructions.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef1#1{{}}\mathdef1{instruction} & \href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_I &::=& \mathit{in}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-plaininstr}{\mathtt{plaininstr}}_I &\Rightarrow& \mathit{in} \\ &&|& \mathit{in}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blockinstr}{\mathtt{blockinstr}}_I &\Rightarrow& \mathit{in} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

In addition, as a syntactic abbreviation, instructions can be written as S-expressions in folded form, to group them visually.

Labels¶

Structured control instructions can be annotated with a symbolic label identifier. They are the only symbolic identifiers that can be bound locally in an instruction sequence. The following grammar handles the corresponding update to the identifier context by composing the context with an additional label entry.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcllll} \def\mathdef1#1{{}}\mathdef1{label} & \href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}_I &::=& v{:}\href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}} &\Rightarrow& \{\href{../text/conventions.html#text-context}{\mathsf{labels}}~v\} \href{../syntax/conventions.html#notation-compose}{\oplus} I & (\mathrel{\mbox{if}} v \notin I.\href{../text/conventions.html#text-context}{\mathsf{labels}}) \\ &&|& \epsilon &\Rightarrow& \{\href{../text/conventions.html#text-context}{\mathsf{labels}}~(\epsilon)\} \href{../syntax/conventions.html#notation-compose}{\oplus} I \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Note

The new label entry is inserted at the beginning of the label list in the identifier context. This effectively shifts all existing labels up by one, mirroring the fact that control instructions are indexed relatively not absolutely.

Control Instructions¶

Structured control instructions can bind an optional symbolic label identifier. The same label identifier may optionally be repeated after the corresponding \(\mathtt{end}\) and \(\mathtt{else}\) pseudo instructions, to indicate the matching delimiters.

Their block type is given as a type use, analogous to the type of functions. However, the special case of a type use that is syntactically empty or consists of only a single result is not regarded as an abbreviation for an inline function type, but is parsed directly into an optional value type.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef1#1{{}}\mathdef1{block type} & \href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}_I & \begin{array}[t]{@{}c@{}} ::= \\ | \\ \end{array} & \begin{array}[t]{@{}lcll@{}} (t{:}\href{../text/types.html#text-functype}{\mathtt{result}})^? &\Rightarrow& t^? \\ x,I'{:}\href{../text/modules.html#text-typeuse}{\mathtt{typeuse}}_I &\Rightarrow& x & (\mathrel{\mbox{if}} I' = \{\}) \\ \end{array} \\ \def\mathdef1#1{{}}\mathdef1{block instruction} & \href{../text/instructions.html#text-blockinstr}{\mathtt{blockinstr}}_I &::=& \def\mathdef40#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef40{block}~~I'{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}_I~~\mathit{bt}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~(\mathit{in}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_{I'})^\ast~~\def\mathdef41#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef41{end}~~\href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}^? \\ &&&\qquad \Rightarrow\quad \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{block}}~\mathit{bt}~\mathit{in}^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}} \qquad\quad~~ (\mathrel{\mbox{if}} \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}^? = \epsilon \vee \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}^? = \href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}) \\ &&|& \def\mathdef42#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef42{loop}~~I'{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}_I~~\mathit{bt}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~(\mathit{in}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_{I'})^\ast~~\def\mathdef43#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef43{end}~~\href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}^? \\ &&&\qquad \Rightarrow\quad \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{loop}}~\mathit{bt}~\mathit{in}^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}} \qquad\qquad (\mathrel{\mbox{if}} \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}^? = \epsilon \vee \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}^? = \href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}) \\ &&|& \def\mathdef44#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef44{if}~~I'{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}_I~~\mathit{bt}{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~(\mathit{in}_1{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_{I'})^\ast~~ \def\mathdef45#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef45{else}~~\href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}_1^?~~(\mathit{in}_2{:}\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_{I'})^\ast~~\def\mathdef46#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef46{end}~~\href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}_2^? \\ &&&\qquad \Rightarrow\quad \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{if}}~\mathit{bt}~\mathit{in}_1^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{else}}~\mathit{in}_2^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}} \qquad (\mathrel{\mbox{if}} \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}_1^? = \epsilon \vee \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}_1^? = \href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}, \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}_2^? = \epsilon \vee \href{../text/values.html#text-id}{\mathtt{id}}_2^? = \href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Note

The side condition stating that the identifier context \(I'\) must be empty in the rule for \(\href{../text/modules.html#text-typeuse}{\mathtt{typeuse}}\) block types enforces that no identifier can be bound in any \(\href{../text/types.html#text-functype}{\mathtt{param}}\) declaration for a block type.

All other control instruction are represented verbatim.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcllll} \def\mathdef1#1{{}}\mathdef1{plain instruction} & \href{../text/instructions.html#text-plaininstr}{\mathtt{plaininstr}}_I &::=& \def\mathdef47#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef47{unreachable} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{unreachable}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef48#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef48{nop} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{nop}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef49#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef49{br}~~l{:}\href{../text/modules.html#text-labelidx}{\mathtt{labelidx}}_I &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{br}}~l \\ &&|& \def\mathdef50#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef50{br\_if}~~l{:}\href{../text/modules.html#text-labelidx}{\mathtt{labelidx}}_I &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{br\_if}}~l \\ &&|& \def\mathdef51#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef51{br\_table}~~l^\ast{:}\href{../text/conventions.html#text-vec}{\mathtt{vec}}(\href{../text/modules.html#text-labelidx}{\mathtt{labelidx}}_I)~~l_N{:}\href{../text/modules.html#text-labelidx}{\mathtt{labelidx}}_I &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{br\_table}}~l^\ast~l_N \\ &&|& \def\mathdef52#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef52{return} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{return}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef53#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef53{call}~~x{:}\href{../text/modules.html#text-funcidx}{\mathtt{funcidx}}_I &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{call}}~x \\ &&|& \def\mathdef54#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef54{call\_indirect}~~x,I'{:}\href{../text/modules.html#text-typeuse}{\mathtt{typeuse}}_I &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{call\_indirect}}~x & (\mathrel{\mbox{if}} I' = \{\}) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Note

The side condition stating that the identifier context \(I'\) must be empty in the rule for \(\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{call\_indirect}}\) enforces that no identifier can be bound in any \(\href{../text/types.html#text-functype}{\mathtt{param}}\) declaration appearing in the type annotation.

Memory Instructions¶

Folded Instructions¶

Instructions can be written as S-expressions by grouping them into folded form. In that notation, an instruction is wrapped in parentheses and optionally includes nested folded instructions to indicate its operands.

In the case of block instructions, the folded form omits the \(\def\mathdef426#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef426{end}\) delimiter. For \(\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{if}}\) instructions, both branches have to be wrapped into nested S-expressions, headed by the keywords \(\def\mathdef427#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef427{then}\) and \(\def\mathdef428#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef428{else}\).

The set of all phrases defined by the following abbreviations recursively forms the auxiliary syntactic class \(\href{../text/instructions.html#text-foldedinstr}{\mathtt{foldedinstr}}\). Such a folded instruction can appear anywhere a regular instruction can.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lllll} \def\mathdef1#1{{}}\mathdef1{instruction} & \def\mathdef429#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef429{(}~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-plaininstr}{\mathtt{plaininstr}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-foldedinstr}{\mathtt{foldedinstr}}^\ast~\def\mathdef430#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef430{)} &\equiv\quad \href{../text/instructions.html#text-foldedinstr}{\mathtt{foldedinstr}}^\ast~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-plaininstr}{\mathtt{plaininstr}} \\ & \def\mathdef431#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef431{(}~\def\mathdef432#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef432{block}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}^\ast~\def\mathdef433#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef433{)} &\equiv\quad \def\mathdef434#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef434{block}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}^\ast~~\def\mathdef435#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef435{end} \\ & \def\mathdef436#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef436{(}~\def\mathdef437#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef437{loop}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}^\ast~\def\mathdef438#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef438{)} &\equiv\quad \def\mathdef439#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef439{loop}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}^\ast~~\def\mathdef440#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef440{end} &\hspace{-3ex} \def\mathdef443#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef443{(}~\def\mathdef444#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef444{then}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_1^\ast~\def\mathdef445#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef445{)}~~\def\mathdef446#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef446{(}~\def\mathdef447#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef447{else}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_2^\ast~\def\mathdef448#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef448{)}^?~~\def\mathdef449#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef449{)} \href{../text/instructions.html#text-foldedinstr}{\mathtt{foldedinstr}}^\ast~~\def\mathdef450#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef450{if}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-label}{\mathtt{label}}~~\href{../text/instructions.html#text-blocktype}{\mathtt{blocktype}} \href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_1^\ast~~\def\mathdef451#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef451{else}~~(\href{../text/instructions.html#text-instr}{\mathtt{instr}}_2^\ast)^?~\def\mathdef452#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef452{end} Note

For example, the instruction sequence

\[\mathtt{(local.get~\$x)~(i32.const~2)~i32.add~(i32.const~3)~i32.mul}\]

can be folded into

\[\mathtt{(i32.mul~(i32.add~(local.get~\$x)~(i32.const~2))~(i32.const~3))}\]

Folded instructions are solely syntactic sugar, no additional syntactic or type-based checking is implied.

Expressions¶

Expressions are written as instruction sequences. No explicit \(\def\mathdef453#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef453{end}\) keyword is included, since they only occur in bracketed positions.
